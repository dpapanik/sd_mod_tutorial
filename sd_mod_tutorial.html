System Dynamics MoD Example

SHARE:
The following stock-flow model represents a shared mobility-on-demand (MoD) system in an idealized city with continuous space. The city consists of two types of regions, A and B, each with different dynamic patterns of inflows, outflows, and accumulations. You can control the parameters of the mixture models of outflows from each region. The model computes the inflows as delays of the outflows and numerically integrates the accumulation dynamics of the two stocks, A and B. The total trip volume remains constant.
Each mixture model consists of three peaks (modes): the first peak represents the morning inbound commuting peak (from households to workplaces); the second peak represents the evening outbound commuting peak (from workplaces back to households); the third peak represents all other types of trips that occur during the day.
Interact with the model and explore the following questions: what combinations of the three peaks cause greater fluctuations in the accumulation dynamics? What type of trip patterns these combinations may refer to? What type of cities or land use mixtures these trip patterns may refer to? How does fleet size and parking land requirements of a shared MoD system relate to commuting patterns?
